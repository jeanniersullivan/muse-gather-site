Here is the whole letter, if you're in a hurry: you only get one life, and most of it happens on ordinary Tuesdays. Make those true, and the rest tends to follow.

We talk about living deliberately as though it were a single grand decision — a move, a quitting, a leap. But the deliberate life is smaller and stranger than that. It is what you do with the twenty minutes before anyone else is awake. It is whether you answer the message now or after you've finished your own sentence.

> Make it true. Not impressive. Not optimized. True.

No one is watching the part that matters, which is precisely why it matters. Choose it anyway.
