I didn't plant them. That's the first thing to say. The wildflowers along the fence came back on their own this spring, while I was busy being certain that nothing good grows without tending.

There is a particular arrogance to thinking a garden needs you. It doesn't, mostly. It needs weather and time and a little benign neglect. I have spent years learning what the fence line already knew: that some things finish themselves if you can bear to stop managing them.

## What the season taught me

Rest is not the reward for the work. Sometimes it is the work. I watched the coreopsis open without my permission and felt something in my chest unclench.

Let it be unfinished. Let it come back on its own. Pay attention, and then — this is the hard part — leave it be.
