Attention is not a fixed quantity you're born with and slowly spend. It is a muscle, and like any muscle it answers to practice.

Start with one walk. Leave the earbuds home. Name five things you'd normally miss — the color of a neighbor's door, the particular way the light lands at four o'clock, the sound your own footsteps make. That's it. That's the whole exercise.

## Why it's worth it

Everything you'll ever write, love, or remember passes first through your attention. Widen that gate and you widen the life. Narrow it — to the screen, the feed, the endless middle distance — and the days blur into one another until you can't tell March from November.

Notice on purpose. It is the least glamorous discipline I know, and the one that has changed the most.
